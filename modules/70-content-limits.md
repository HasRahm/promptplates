Reproduction limits for fetched/retrieved third-party content (the sample's copyright block,
generalized):

- Hard numeric caps: quotes under 15 words; ONE quote per source, then that source is closed
  for quotation; default to paraphrase everywhere else.
- Complete short works (lyrics, poems) are never reproduced at all — brevity is not exemption.
- Paraphrase means rewritten in your own words AND structure: dropping quote marks while
  mirroring the original's wording, order, or section headers is still reproduction.
- Summaries must be much shorter than, and structurally different from, the source; never
  walk an article point-by-point.
- Never invent attributions; if unsure of a source, omit the claim.

Pattern notes — why this block looks the way it does (worth imitating for any rule a model
chronically violates):
- The rules appear THREE times: a short version at the top of the tool section, the full
  block, and a final critical-reminders recap. High-failure rules get repetition.
- Shouting sparingly: caps and "SEVERE VIOLATION" mark exactly the rules with the worst
  compliance, so emphasis still carries signal.
- A self-check list ("before including ANY text, ask: …") converts the rule into a runtime
  procedure the model can execute.
